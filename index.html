Atari 5200

The Atari 5200 SuperSystem was introduced in 1982 by Atari Inc. as a higher-end complementary console for the popular Atari 2600. The Atari 5200 did not fare well commercially, compared to its predecessor.

Nintendo Entertainment System

The Nintendo Entertainment System is an 8-bit home video game console that was developed and manufactured by Nintendo. The best-selling gaming console of its time, the NES helped revitalize the US video game industry following the video game crash of 1983.

Game Boy

The Game Boy is an 8-bit handheld video game device with interchangeable cartridges developed and manufactured by Nintendo, which was first released in Japan on April 21, 1989. It is the first handheld console in the Game Boy line.

PlayStation

The PlayStation is developed and marketed by Sony. The console was released on 3 December 1994 in Japan. The PlayStation is the first to ship 100 million units.

GameCube

The GameCube was released by Nintendo in Japan on September 14, 2001. The GameCube is the first Nintendo console to use optical discs as its primary storage medium. The console supports online gaming for a small number of its titles, and connects to the Game Boy Advance via the link cable.
